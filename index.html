Earthen Joy restaurant opened in Tempe, Arizona about 40 years ago. It was located at 36 east 5th Street in Tempe, Arizona near the Gentle Strength Co-op and quickly helped define the Tempe lifestyle of the 1970s.

The little restaurant was created by Culver and Wylie van Buren (center left above). Its reputation for natural food with an epicurean flair quickly grew, as did the family of individuals that work there.

I was the first employee and worked at Earthen Joy for most of its life.

I'm compiling information and remembrances of Earthen Joy, its stories, and its impact on the lives of those who ate there.

Who of you remember the Bilbo Baggins and Gandalf Supreme, or the Sheepherder with its chunky tomato vegetable soup and little whole wheat loaf from Christopher's Bakery on Mill Avenue? Do you remember the blender drinks, like the Elmer Fudd? How about the evening specials like the Coquille Saint Jacques served in a scallop shell. And was anyone able to escape being enchanted by Wylie's carob cake and carob sauce?

If you would like to share photos, stories or thoughts, we can add them to this site.

If you worked at, or remember Earthen Joy, I'd appreciate hearing from you.
